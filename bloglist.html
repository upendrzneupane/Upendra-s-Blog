Nepali Men's National Cricket Team:

Nepal have been participating in international cricket since 1996 when they competed in the 1996 ACC
Trophy. They have competed in numerous tournaments over the years including the ICC Trophy (later the
ICC World Cup Qualifier), the ICC Intercontinental Cup.


Pashupatinath Temple:

Pashupatinath Temple is the oldest Hindu temple in Kathmandu. It is not known for certain when
Pashupatinath Temple was built. But according to Nepal Mahatmaya and Himvatkhanda, the deity here
gained great fame there as Pashupati. Pashupatinath Temple's existence is recorded as early as 400 CE.


Bhaktapur Durbar Square:

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a former royal palace complex in Bhaktapur, Nepal. It's a UNESCO World
Heritage Site and is considered one of the best places to visit in Bhaktapur.


Rhododendron:

Rhododendron arboreum, also known as Lali gurans in Nepali, is Nepal's national flower. It's an evergreen
shrub or small tree with bright red flowers that are usually tubular, bell-shaped, or funnel-shaped. The
flowers are often fragrant and can be a sign of spring.


Gosaikunda:

Gosaikunda is a freshwater lake in Nepal's Langtang National Park. It's located in the Rasuwa District at
an elevation of 14,370 ft. The lake is 34 acres in size and is known as Silu among the Newars.


Mount Everest:

Mount Everest is the tallest mountain in the world at 29,032 feet (8,849 meters) above sea level. It's located
in the Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and Tibet, an autonomous region of China.


Phewa Lake:

Phewa Lake is a freshwater lake in Nepal, located in the Pokhara Valley in the western part of the country.
It's the second largest lake in Nepal and the largest in Gandaki Province, at an altitude of 742 meters.


National Flag of Nepal:

The flag is a simplified combination of two single pennons (or pennants), known as a double-pennon. Its
crimson red is the symbol of bravery and it also represents the color of the rhododendron, Nepal's national
flower, while the blue border is the color of peace.
